Intro

I am a student at Boston University majoring in Physics, minoring in Computer Science and Philosophy. I am interested in the intersection of art, physics, and philosophy. I am currently working on a 2D indie game while learning 3D art on blender, check out my work.

In my free time, I like to hike, read, or act in student films on campus.

research

I have been involved with biophysics research throughout undergrad under Professor Erramilli at Boston University. I received funding from UROP to conduct a project and present at a symposium in October 2022. Here is my full poster. I was involved in implementation and evaluation of the experiment, collaborating with graduate student Ramprasath Rajagopal. Our experiment was a pivot from using a fluorescence marker (to study the temperature properties of the shockwave model of gold nanorods) to studying the degradation of the fluorescence by reactive oxygen species generated by irradiated gold nano rods.

As an intern in Advanced Laboratory (Class Code: PY581), I conducted an experiment with my collaborator Zachary Klappenbach studying chaotic periods of water droplets. We used an oscilloscope and photodiodes coordinated by LABVIEW in our setup. Here is my paper.

In Advanced Laboratory (Class Code: PY581), my collaborator Isabelle Boegholm and I measured the crystal structure of different materials using X-ray crystallography with the Tel-X-Ometer. Here is my paper.

projects

As part of Break Through Tech AI, I worked with 3 undergraduate students to collect climate and bat species data and design a predictive AI model characterizing the spread of White Nose Syndrome in bat species across North America for BioInterphase's engineering initiative on this issue. We presented to stakeholders and our challenge advisor, Noah Snyder, the CEO of BioInterphase, in Dec 2023.

In MIT's Policy Hackathon (2023), my team of 4 undergraduates researched and wrote a policy memo for the housing sustainability challenge put forth by Princeton's Eviction Lab.

I published a platformer game called Dream Catcher on itch.io: Dream Catcher. I used the Unity platform and C# to put it together, drawing the animations, sprites, and tiles on procreate, while making the sound effects on Audity.

The 2D indie game I am working on is inspired by an August Moon Festival myth and my childhood of looking over my brother's shoulder, watching him play Pokemon Black & White on his Nintendo DS.

In a Directed Reading Program with the University Math Department (2021), I worked through several chapters of A Friendly Introdution to Number Theory and wrote a script to do RSA Encryption and Decryption. The functions and scripts I wrote are on github.
